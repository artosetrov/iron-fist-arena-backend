# Hexbound — World, Lore & Narrative

*Setting, tone, and world-building reference. Updated: 2026-03-21*

---

## World Overview

**Setting:** A grimdark dark fantasy world in decay — cursed lands, fallen kingdoms, forgotten gods. The world of Hexbound is not post-apocalyptic in a sci-fi sense — it is post-cataclysm in a medieval/fantasy sense. Something ancient and terrible happened, and the world never recovered.

**Time Period:** Roughly late-medieval / early-Renaissance technology level. No gunpowder. Magic exists but is dangerous, unpredictable, and often corrupted.

**Tone:** Bleak, dangerous, morally grey. There are no clear "good guys." Survival, ambition, and power are the driving forces. Think Darkest Dungeon meets Dark Souls meets old-school D&D modules.

---

## The Hex

The world is cursed. The curse is called **The Hex** — an ancient magical corruption that seeps into everything: land, creatures, people, artifacts. Nobody knows its origin. Some say it's the dying breath of a dead god. Others say it was always there, just dormant.

### Effects of The Hex

- **On the land:** Stone crumbles, forests rot, water turns black. Safe zones exist but are shrinking.
- **On creatures:** Wildlife becomes monstrous — cave spiders, plague bearers, bone constructs.
- **On people:** Prolonged exposure warps the body and mind. Some gain power (magic users), most lose everything.
- **On objects:** Artifacts and equipment become "hexbound" — powerful but dangerous. The best gear in the game is cursed, which explains why all equipment looks aged, corroded, and sinister.

### Why Players Fight

The Hex drives conflict. Resources are scarce, safe zones are contested, and the only way to grow stronger is to fight — other players, dungeon monsters, and bosses who are deeply hexbound creatures. PvP isn't abstract — it's survival competition in a dying world.

## Races (Origins)

Each origin has a distinct relationship with The Hex:

| Origin | Relationship to The Hex | Flavor |
|--------|------------------------|--------|
| **Human** | Resist it through willpower and community | Most numerous, most desperate. Build walls, forge iron, pray to dead gods. |
| **Orc** | Embrace it as strength | Believe The Hex is natural. The strong survive. War is worship. |
| **Skeleton** | Are products of it | Reanimated dead. Some retain memories, most don't. Serve or wander. |
| **Demon** | Feed on it | Drawn to hexbound zones. Gain power from corruption. Feared by all. |
| **Dogfolk** | Navigate around it | Nomadic, cunning. Avoid the worst hex zones, trade between settlements. |

## Classes (Combat Roles)

Classes reflect how characters survive in the hexbound world:

| Class | Archetype | Lore Justification |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Warrior** | Brute force fighter | In a world where everything is trying to kill you, hit harder than it. |
| **Rogue** | Opportunist, survivor | Speed and cunning keep you alive longer than armor. Strike fast, vanish. |
| **Mage** | Hex-channeler | The brave (or insane) who channel The Hex itself as magical power. High reward, high risk. |
| **Tank** | Living wall | Someone has to hold the line. Endurance and sacrifice over glory. |

## Key Locations (Flavor Framework)

These are the aesthetic and thematic zones that inform dungeon design, backgrounds, and art prompts:

### The Rotting City (Hub)
The player's home base. A crumbling stone city that still functions — barely. Markets run, guards patrol, but everything is held together with desperation and iron will. This is where the Hub screen, shops, and social features live.

### The Undercrofts (Dungeons)
Beneath the surface: crypts, sewers, abandoned mines, collapsed temples. Each dungeon floor is deeper into the earth, closer to the source of The Hex. Bosses are ancient hexbound creatures that guard something valuable.

### The Arena (PvP)
A gladiatorial pit where warriors prove their worth. Not lawless — it has rules, referees (the ELO system), and spectators. Fighting in the Arena is the fastest way to earn gold, reputation, and respect.

### The Outlands (Future Content)
Beyond the city walls: blasted heathlands, toxic swamps, petrified forests. Reserved for future open-world or exploration content.

## Narrative Approach

Hexbound is **not a story-driven game**. It's a systems-driven PvP RPG with lore as flavor. The narrative serves the systems, not the other way around.

### How Lore Appears

- **Item descriptions:** Short flavor text on equipment, explaining what it was before The Hex claimed it
- **Dungeon descriptions:** Brief text introducing each dungeon zone and its boss
- **Character origin text:** A few sentences during character creation about each origin
- **Achievement names:** Lore-flavored titles ("Hex Breaker," "Bone Collector," "Undercroft Survivor")
- **Boss names and abilities:** Every boss has a name and theme rooted in the world

### How Lore Does NOT Appear

- No cutscenes
- No dialogue trees
- No quest storylines (quests are system-driven: "Win 3 PvP matches," not "Save the princess")
- No NPC conversations
- No journal/codex system (unless added later)

This is intentional. Lore adds atmosphere without consuming development time or player session time (remember: 2-5 minute sessions).

## Tone Guide for Writing

When writing any in-game text (item descriptions, dungeon intros, achievement names, boss titles):

- **Short.** 1-2 sentences max. Players don't read long text on mobile.
- **Bleak but not edgy.** "The blade weeps rust" not "The blade DRIPS WITH THE BLOOD OF INNOCENT SOULS."
- **Concrete, not abstract.** "Forged in a dead city's last furnace" not "Imbued with the essence of darkness."
- **No humor.** The world is dying. Irony is okay, jokes are not.
- **Consistent vocabulary:** hex, hexbound, cursed, corroded, ancient, forgotten, shattered, crumbling, rotting, rusted.

### Examples

**Item descriptions:**
- Rusted Longsword: "Pulled from a skeleton's grip. Still sharp enough."
- Hexbound Shield: "The wood has turned black. It absorbs more than it deflects."
- Bone Helm: "The previous owner didn't need it anymore."

**Dungeon intros:**
- The Sunken Crypt: "Three levels below the city's foundation. The water here glows faintly purple."
- The Iron Foundry: "Once produced the city's finest weapons. Now produces something else entirely."

**Boss names:**
- Corpse Weaver (spider boss)
- The Hollow King (undead knight)
- Plague Bearer (poison boss)
- Iron Sentinel (golem boss)

## Art Style Connection

The visual art style directly reflects the lore:
- **Pen and ink illustration** = medieval manuscript feel, like records of a dying world
- **Muted earth tones + saturated accents** = a gray world where only magic/danger has color
- **Grimdark aesthetic** = everything is decayed, corroded, ancient
- **Isolated on white background** = game sprites, not story illustrations

## Future Lore Expansion Hooks

If narrative content is ever prioritized, these hooks are ready:

1. **Who created The Hex?** — Mystery quest chain or seasonal event
2. **Origin-specific storylines** — Each race has a different relationship with The Hex
3. **Dungeon lore progression** — Deeper floors reveal more about the world's past
4. **Boss backstories** — Each boss was once something else before The Hex
5. **Legendary item stories** — Rarest items have multi-paragraph origin stories
6. **Guild lore** — When guilds are added, they can have lore-based factions

These require zero system changes — they're pure content additions.
