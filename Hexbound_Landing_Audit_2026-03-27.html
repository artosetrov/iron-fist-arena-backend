2. Strengths (What's Working)

Visual Atmosphere

Dark theme with gold accents, GSAP scroll animations, parallax backgrounds, particle effects, custom cursor glow — the page feels like entering a game world. Color palette is consistent with the iOS app. This is the site's biggest asset and shouldn't be lost in a redesign.

Content Depth

Lore section with actual worldbuilding, full class/race/dungeon showcases, bestiary with 16 monsters — shows genuine game depth. Most indie games show 3 screenshots and a download link. Hexbound shows the whole world.

Fair Play Messaging

"Strategy Beats Spending" parallax break + Free vs Premium comparison table is excellent positioning. In a market where players distrust F2P games, this directly addresses the #1 objection. Keep and amplify this.

Technical Craft

Single-file HTML with no framework overhead. GSAP animations are performant. Lazy loading on images. Responsive breakpoints at 1024/768/480px. Scroll progress bar. Back-to-top button. Mobile menu. The craft is solid.

FAQ Quality

The 6 FAQ items are well-chosen and address real player concerns: pay-to-win, platforms, matchmaking, offline play, prestige, session length. Answers are specific with real numbers (not vague marketing).

3. Problems & Weak Points

Critical No Video / Gameplay Footage

This is the single biggest problem. Pages with video convert 86% higher. Every major competitor (RAID, Diablo Immortal, AFK Journey) leads with cinematic or gameplay trailers. Hexbound has zero video content — only static images and icons. A player cannot visualize what the game actually looks like in action.

Impact: Massive conversion loss. Visitors can't judge if the game is fun.

Critical No Real Screenshots

The "Screenshots" section shows 5 phone mockups with background images (bg-hub.jpg, bg-arena.jpg, etc.) — these are environment art, not actual UI screenshots. A visitor sees dark atmospheric blobs, not the actual game interface with buttons, menus, combat, and characters.

Impact: Trust deficit. Looks like the game doesn't exist yet.

Critical Fake Social Proof

The "Press" strip lists TouchArcade, Pocket Gamer, IGN Mobile, App Store Editors' Pick, RPG Fan — but with zero links, zero quotes, and zero evidence. If these are real mentions, they need real links. If not, remove them entirely. Players and journalists WILL notice and it destroys credibility.

The "Reviews" section has obviously fabricated usernames (ShadowReaper_X, IronWall_99, VoidWalker) with no link to actual App Store reviews. This reads as fake.

Impact: Trust destroyed. Looks dishonest.

High iOS-Only CTA

Every download button links to App Store only. No Google Play button, no "coming soon" for Android, no pre-register option, no email capture for future platforms. This means 50%+ of mobile traffic (Android users) has zero conversion path. They leave with nothing.

Impact: Half of all visitors have no action to take.

High No Email Capture / Community Funnel

There is zero lead capture anywhere on the page. No email signup, no Discord join CTA, no newsletter. Social icons (Twitter, Discord, Reddit) all link to # — literally nowhere. If a visitor isn't ready to download right now, there's no way to re-engage them.

Impact: Every non-download visitor is permanently lost.

High Too Long — Content Fatigue

The page has 17+ sections with ~1,440 lines of HTML. Lore + Origins + Features + Screenshots + How It Works + Classes + Rarity + Dungeons + Bestiary + Stats + Reviews + Comparison + FAQ + CTA. That's a LOT of scrolling. By the Rarity Tiers section, most visitors have bounced. Sections like Rarity and Bestiary are enthusiast-level detail that hurt conversion by burying the CTA.

Impact: Scroll depth drops. Final CTA rarely seen.

Medium Preloader Adds Unnecessary Wait

2.4-second preloader with logo + loading bar. Users expect instant pages. The preloader exists for "atmosphere" but actually increases bounce rate. Modern best practice: show content immediately, animate elements in as they load.

Medium Hero Section — All Text, No Visual Hook

The hero shows: logo → tagline → description text → 5 pill badges → App Store button. No character art, no game footage, no visual "hook" that shows what the game IS. Compare to RAID (massive hero character + cinematic video) or Diablo Immortal (full-bleed animated hero with class showcase).

Medium Class Icons Are Tiny & Generic

The Classes section shows 90x90px icons for Warrior/Rogue/Mage/Tank. These are the same small icons used in the iOS app's UI. On a marketing page, classes should be showcased with full character art, abilities preview, and gameplay clips — not thumbnail-size icons with 2-line descriptions.

Low Footer Links Broken

Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, Cookie Policy all link to #. Social media links all #. Legal pages exist at /privacy and /support (per vercel.json) but aren't linked properly.

4. Competitor Analysis

Analyzed landing pages of major dark fantasy / RPG mobile games. Hexbound competes in perception even if it's indie — players compare your site to AAA marketing.

RAID: Shadow Legends

raidshadowlegends.com

Full-bleed hero with cinematic character art. Auto-playing trailer in hero section. 800+ champions showcased with filterable gallery. News/updates feed on homepage. Multi-platform download (iOS, Android, PC). "Play Free" CTA on every scroll point. Press quotes with actual publication logos. Community hub with Discord/Reddit/YouTube integration. Player count social proof ("65M+ Players").

Key Takeaway Video + massive social proof + multi-platform = conversion machine.

Diablo Immortal

diabloimmortal.blizzard.com

Animated hero with dark atmospheric art. Class carousel with interactive switching (9 classes, each with video preview). "New Content Every 2 Weeks" trust banner. 30+ hours campaign highlighted. Multi-platform (iOS, Android, PC). Battle.net integration. Gameplay showcases as auto-playing short clips per section. Minimal text — visual storytelling. News feed with patch notes.

Key Takeaway Interactive class showcase + auto-playing gameplay clips + minimal copy = immersive.

AFK Journey

afkjourney.lilith.com

Storybook art style hero with hand-drawn quality. Character showcase with animated idle poses. Open world exploration preview with scrolling map. "Idle + Active" gameplay contrast highlighted. Pre-registration bonus system. Email + platform notification signup. Discord community CTA prominent. App Store feature badges (Editor's Choice). Download counter visible.

Key Takeaway Pre-register funnel + animated characters + community CTA = engagement loop.

UnGodly

ungodlygame.com

Dark fantasy aesthetic similar to Hexbound. Character-centric hero with "Primals" showcase. Real-time combat clips embedded. Mobile + PC positioning. Community links prominent (Discord). Simpler page structure — focuses on core gameplay loop. Character collection gallery with rarity system. Less text, more visuals.

Key Takeaway Direct competitor — simpler page, more visual, gameplay video front-and-center.

Common Patterns Across All Competitors

  1. Video in hero section — trailer or gameplay footage above the fold (100% of competitors)
  2. Character art, not icons — full-body illustrations or 3D renders of characters (100%)
  3. Multi-platform CTAs — iOS + Android + PC download buttons (100%)
  4. Real social proof — player counts, press logos with links, App Store ratings (100%)
  5. Community funnels — Discord, email signup, newsletter, pre-register (100%)
  6. Interactive elements — class switchers, character galleries, playable demos (75%)
  7. News/updates feed — shows the game is alive and actively developed (75%)
  8. Short copy, big visuals — visual storytelling over text walls (100%)

6. Upgrade Plan — 3 Phases

PHASE 1 — Quick Wins (1-2 days)

Fix Critical Trust & Conversion Issues

Zero-risk changes that can be shipped immediately with existing assets.

1.1 Remove or Fix Fake Social Proof

  • Press strip: Remove TouchArcade/IGN/etc. names entirely OR replace with actual App Store rating badge + download count (fetch via App Store API or show a static "4.X stars on App Store" badge)
  • Reviews: Replace fabricated reviews with real App Store reviews (screenshot embeds or use App Store review API). Or remove the section entirely until real reviews accumulate.

1.2 Add Android "Coming Soon" + Email Capture

  • Next to every App Store button, add a "Google Play — Coming Soon" button with "Notify Me" that captures email
  • Add a simple email signup section before the footer: "Get notified when Hexbound launches on Android and new features drop"
  • Use a simple service (Mailchimp, ConvertKit, or even a Supabase edge function that stores emails in DB)

1.3 Fix Broken Links

  • Footer Privacy Policy → /privacy
  • Footer Terms of Service → /support (or create a /terms page)
  • Social media links → actual profiles (create Discord server, Twitter/X account if not existing)

1.4 Remove Preloader

  • Delete the preloader entirely. Show content immediately with staggered GSAP entrance animations. The 2.4s delay costs bounce rate.

1.5 Real Screenshots in Phone Mockups

  • Take actual iPhone screenshots from the app: Hub map, Arena battle, Dungeon room, Hero equipment screen, Shop
  • Replace the bg-*.jpg environment art in the Screenshots section with these real captures
  • This is the fastest way to show the game is real and polished
PHASE 2 — Hero Overhaul + Video (3-5 days)

Transform the Hero Section Into a Conversion Machine

The hero section decides 80% of whether a visitor stays or bounces. Currently it's text-heavy with no visual hook.

2.1 Hero Video Background

  • Record 15-30 seconds of gameplay: arena combat, dungeon exploration, hub navigation, loot drops
  • Edit into a seamless loop with dark color grading to match the site palette
  • Play as muted auto-looping background behind the hero content (like RAID does)
  • Fallback: for mobile, show a high-quality static frame from the video (saves bandwidth)
  • Host on Cloudflare R2 or similar CDN for fast loading

2.2 Hero Layout Redesign

  • Above the fold must contain: (1) Video/visual hook, (2) Logo, (3) One-line tagline, (4) App Store rating badge, (5) Download CTA, (6) "Watch Trailer" CTA
  • Remove the 5 pill badges from hero — they're feature details, not hooks. Move to marquee or lower.
  • Add a prominently displayed character — full-body warrior or boss art alongside the logo
  • Two CTAs: primary "Download Free" + secondary "Watch Trailer" (opens a 30-60s trailer modal/video)

2.3 Dedicated Gameplay Trailer

  • 30-60 second trailer: lore intro (3s) → character creation (5s) → hub exploration (5s) → arena combat with abilities firing (15s) → dungeon boss fight (10s) → loot reveal (5s) → logo + "Download Free" end card (5s)
  • This can be screen-recorded from an iPhone and edited with After Effects / DaVinci Resolve. Add epic music, match the dark fantasy vibe.
  • Embed in a "Watch Trailer" modal that opens from the hero CTA
  • Also add as a YouTube embed in the body for SEO

2.4 Interactive Class Showcase

  • Replace the 4 static class cards with an interactive tabbed/switchable component
  • Each class tab shows: full character art (large), 3-4 key abilities with icons, a gameplay clip (5s GIF/WebM of combat), and role description
  • Diablo Immortal's class carousel is the gold standard to emulate

2.5 Condense Page Length

  • Remove: Rarity Tiers section (too niche), second parallax break (redundant)
  • Merge: Bestiary into Dungeons section (show 4-5 key bosses within dungeon cards)
  • Move: Stats counter into the hero section or marquee (more impactful higher up)
  • Target: 12 sections max (from current 17+)
PHASE 3 — Full Immersion Upgrade (1-2 weeks)

Create the Best Indie RPG Landing Page on the Market

These changes transform the page from "good indie site" to "AAA-feeling experience."

3.1 Scroll-Driven Storytelling

  • Instead of discrete sections, create a narrative scroll experience: "Enter the realm" → camera moves through the world as you scroll
  • Use GSAP ScrollTrigger to pin/animate elements as the user scrolls. Example: the Hub map appears as an interactive 3D-ish parallax scene, then transitions to arena, then dungeon
  • Reference: Apple product pages (AirPods Pro, MacBook Pro) for scroll-driven storytelling

3.2 Animated Character Models

  • Create idle animation loops for each class (WebM or Lottie). Show characters breathing, weapons shimmering, capes flowing.
  • On the hero section, show a slowly rotating character spotlight
  • On class showcase, switch characters with cross-fade animation on tab click

3.3 Sound Design (Optional Toggle)

  • Ambient dark fantasy music that plays when user enables it (click speaker icon in nav). Auto-muted by default.
  • Subtle sound effects on hover/click for buttons (sword cling, rune activation)
  • This is what separates "web page" from "game portal". Very few competitors do this.

3.4 Community / Discord Integration

  • Embed live Discord member count: "5,234 warriors online now — Join the Guild"
  • Show community screenshots or fan art gallery
  • News/Updates section: Last 3 patch notes or events, showing the game is alive

3.5 App Store Rating Widget

  • Auto-fetch and display real App Store rating + review count
  • Show in hero section next to download button: "★★★★★ 4.8 — 2.3K Ratings"

3.6 Localization

  • Add Russian language toggle (your primary audience). Even just the hero section + CTA + FAQ in Russian would boost conversion for RU traffic significantly.

3.7 Performance Optimization

  • Convert all PNG assets to WebP (40-60% size reduction)
  • Lazy-load GSAP — defer scroll animations until after first paint
  • Add <link rel="preload"> for hero background and logo
  • Target: Lighthouse Performance > 90 on mobile
